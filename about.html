About Mike

Michael “Mike” Gillespie is a television, web and radio personality from Gainesville, Florida. He currently serves as a play-by-play announcer for Gator Sports Network’s production of Florida Gators softball and hosts University of Florida lacrosse at Donald R. Dizney Stadium. Mike also leads as a sports anchor and reporter at Florida’s public and commercial broadcasting stations.

Gillespie joined the Gator Sports Network in 2009 as an announcer for the University of Florida Gator softball and lacrosse teams. He served as the radio play-by-play announcer for the Florida Gator lacrosse team from 2009-2012, announcing the 2012 ALC Championship Game in Evanston, Illinois. In 2013, Gillespie was named the official public address announcer for University of Florida lacrosse at Donald R. Dizney Stadium.

Gillespie enters his fifth season as an announcer for Florida Gators softball, announcing over 100 softball games in his career with the Network. He calls mostly non-conference games and the beginning of the Southeastern Conference schedule on the radio and GatorVision.

Mike spent summer 2013 in New York City, where held two internships. He split his weeks between CBS Sports Radio Network/WFAN as an intern and SportsNet New York (SNY) where he reported on notable sports events including the 2013 Alex Rodriguez biogenesis scandal, the appointment of Jason Kidd as the Brooklyn Nets head coach and the 2013 MLB All-Star Game.
